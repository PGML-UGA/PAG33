About the Workshop

New directions in crop production and improvement are being steered by the intersection of climate change and market-based programs placing tangible value on ecosystem services (such as carbon capture or water quality), changing the products for which agricultural producers are rewarded. The proposer seeks to encourage, and disseminate results of, genomic research that will contribute to innovative solutions to the critical food-energy-water (FEW) problem of sustainable agricultural intensification, in an environment of climate change and in anticipation of availability to agriculture of a much smaller portion of the world's fresh water than the estimated ~70% which it has grown accustomed to using.

Examples of salient research topics include genomics of existing crops or new domesticates toward goals such as breeding for novel production systems (e.g., perennial grains), or genomics of "orphan" crops cultivated locally and/or on limited scale that offer novel ecosystem services and/or impact on food security. Also appropriate are studies that elucidate the fundamental nature of genes and genomes of plants and their many symbionts in the 'phytoecosystem', for example constituents of the plant microbiome with mutualistic, commensalistic or parasitic relationships; or plant pollinators such as honeybees that are indispensable to plant reproductive fitness and agricultural productivity.
