Call for ~20‑minute talks

We welcome proposals spanning genomics of existing crops and new domesticates, ecosystem‑service‑oriented breeding (e.g., perennial grains), orphan crops with local impact, and the phytoecosystem: plant–microbe interactions and pollination biology. Talks should emphasize innovative solutions to sustainable intensification under climate change and evolving market incentives for ecosystem services.

Focus areas

  • Perennial grain systems; multi‑functional agroecosystems
  • Orphan/local crops delivering carbon or water‑quality services
  • Genomic dissection of plant–microbiome mutualism/antagonism
  • Pollination genomics (e.g., honeybee biology and crop fitness)
  • Foundational genome science enabling ecosystem service gains

About the workshop

New directions in crop production are being shaped by climate change and market programs that place tangible value on ecosystem services (e.g., carbon capture, water quality). Reward structures are shifting, and genomic insights can accelerate solutions across the food‑energy‑water nexus while agriculture adapts to reduced freshwater availability.

We seek to encourage and disseminate results across crops and their phytoecosystems—including crop wild relatives, microbial partners (mutualists, commensals, parasites), and essential pollinators.
